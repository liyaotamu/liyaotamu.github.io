Effect of the continental shelf slope around island on the Island Rule

Lixin Qu* , Xiaopei Lin
May 2014
PDF

Abstract

Recent observations demonstrate the transport and heat advection of the Kuroshio Current have been increasing during the past decades. However, the global pattern of wind stress implies that the wind stress curl at the latitudes of the Kuroshio Current region decreases during the past 30 years. This contrasts to the classical Island Rule theory, which predicts that the transport of the Kuroshio Current should be proportional to the wind stress curl at the latitudes of this region. This study attempts to address this discrepancy by adapting the classical theory to the scenario with a continental shelf slope around an island. Numerical experiments based on a barotropic model are also conducted to investigate the dynamical mechanism. The primary finding is that the wind stress curl at the higher latitudes controls the transport around the island rather than the one at the same latitudes as demonstrated in the classical theory. The physical mechanism is dealing with the propagation of Rossby waves. The waveguide of the Rossby waves is modified by the topographic beta effect of the shelf such that the modulating signals are in fact coming from the higher latitudes than the island region. Consequently, the transport around an island is regulated by the wind at higher latitudes in the scenario with a continental shelf around the island.
