Submesoscale baroclinic instabilities

Apr 27, 2016

Coastal buoyancy-driven flows are often associated with stronger lateral density gradients than open ocean fronts, but are seldom observed to be associated with instabilities; in particular, baroclinic instabilities are seldom observed in river plumes, even though lateral buoyancy gradients within the fronts are strong. That contrasts the classical quasi-geostrophic (QG) theory of buoyancy-driven flow over a sloping bottom that predicts unconditional instability growth – the QG assumption may not be reasonable in submesoscale, energetic coastal fronts. We extend the classical non-geostrophic theory to the scenario with a sloping bottom; the adapted theory predicts the suppression of instabilities and indicates that the slope Burger number is the parameter controlling the suppression.
